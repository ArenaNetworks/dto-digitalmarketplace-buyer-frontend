What happens at the evaluation stage

After your brief closes find the seller that best meets your needs by:

  1. Evaluating sellers using the criteria you specified in your brief.
  2. Scoring sellers using the scoring template.

You can then award a contract to the applicant who meets your needs and has the highest score.

Make the evaluation process fair, open and transparent

  • Ask sellers to provide information in the same format.
  • Wait until the brief closes to look at seller responses.
  • Score all sellers against the same criteria and the same scoring template.
  • Record all sellers scores.
  • Use the same evaluation team for each assessment method.
  • Give feedback to each seller after you’ve made a decision.

What you can evaluate on

  • Technical competence — how well the seller’s skills or proposal meet your needs
  • Cultural fit — how the seller will work in your organisation
  • Price - whether the proposal or individual represents value for money.

The evaluation process

  1. Plan the methods and the team of people you’ll use to assess sellers.
  2. Invite shortlisted seller to be assessed. For example, ask for a resume from individual specialist or a proposal for providing an outcome.
  3. Evaluate sellers using the methods you published with your brief, taking notes as you go.
  4. Score sellers using a scoring template and evidence from your notes.
  5. Meet with team members who are evaluating sellers to agree an overall score for each seller.
  6. Award a contract to the seller with the highest score and give feedback to unsuccessful sellers.

What to keep a record of

Keep a record of how you’ve made your decisions and all communication with sellers.

Where to get help

You can get help assessing technical competence from:

  • digital experts in your agency
  • the procurement team in your agency
  • government digital communities of practice
  • experts outside your organisation
