Selling through the Digital Marketplace

New sellers - if you'd like to be part of the Digital Marketplace, register your interest by emailing marketplace@digital.gov.au.

Who can sell

Currently, all sellers in the Marketplace are part of the Digital Service Professionals Panel. The panel was created through an open approach to market by the Digital Transformation Office. If you're interested in joining the Marketplace, or want to expand the services you already offer through the Marketplace, contact marketplace@digital.gov.au and we’ll let you know when you can apply to join.

How to sell

Government buyers publish opportunities to the Marketplace by creating a brief for an individual specialist or a brief for a team to provide an outcome. As a seller you are encouraged to apply for any of these briefs if you have the skills, experience and availability required.

Check the opportunities regularly as they’re only open for 1 or 2 weeks.

How to apply for a brief

When viewing the latest opportunities if there are any that interest you, and you have the necessary skills and availability, select ‘Start application’ and follow the prompts to complete your application. When applying for an individual specialist brief, the quote for your day rate can’t exceed the price you have agreed to for the Digital Service Professionals Panel.

When applying, you’ll be asked whether you have the essential and nice-to-have skills and experience specified on that brief. You must have all essential skills and experience to apply for the brief.

If the brief is for an individual specialist, you also need to be approved to offer the capability that’s required in the brief, you can check the your approved capabilities by viewing your business in the seller catalogue.

Asking questions

If you have any questions about a brief, you can ask them by selecting sign in to ask a question’ and posting your question as directed. Your question will then be emailed by the Marketplace to the buyer to answer. Your question and the buyer’s answer will then be posted on the brief by the buyer.

Question and answer sessions

Buyers may also offer a question and answer session for potential sellers. You’ll need to sign in to view the details of how to join a question and answer session.

What's next

After the brief closes you will be informed by the buyer whether or not you have been shortlisted.

Shortlisted sellers are then invited into the evaluation stage to select the successful seller. The buyer will provide details of the evaluation if you’re successfully shortlisted.

Accessing the Marketplace

All sellers in the Marketplace will be provided with the details to login to the Marketplace. You'll also be able to add additional contributors so other people in your company can access your account.

If you have any difficulties logging into the Marketplace, contact the support desk by emailing marketplace@digital.gov.au.

Contact us

The Digital Marketplace is an agile development in its beta stage. This means the site will evolve over time in response to user needs. If you have any questions, feedback or would like to be involved in user research, email us at marketplace@digital.gov.au.
