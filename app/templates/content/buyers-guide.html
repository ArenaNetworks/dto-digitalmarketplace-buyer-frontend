Buying from the Digital Marketplace

Digital Marketplace overview

The Digital Marketplace makes it easy to engage digital specialist services. As an agile development, the Marketplace will expand over time to offer more services, more sellers and ways to do business.

What can you buy

Digital specialists

These are individual specialists with the skills essential to deliver today’s digital projects. Find out more about the capabilities available, including guidance on the rates you might expect to pay.

Outcomes

If you need a team of digital specialists with the expertise to deliver an outcome, for example a booking system or a website redesign and development.

About the sellers

There are more than 200 sellers in the Digital Marketplace who can deliver professional services and outcomes for digital projects. You’ll need to make your own assessment about whether a seller has the skills, experience and suitability for your own project.

All sellers in the Digital Marketplace are members of the Digital Service Professionals Panel (SON3364729) and have accepted the Marketplace’s Deed of Standing Offer. This deed provides the basis of any contract you enter for services procured through the Marketplace. This panel was established through an open approach to market by the Digital Transformation Office.

How to find and engage sellers for your digital project

There are 2 ways to engage sellers on the Digital Marketplace.

Publish a brief

Publish a brief of your requirements and evaluation criteria in the Marketplace for 1 or 2 weeks. Sellers can apply online if they meet the essential evaluation criteria you’ve set, and are available when you need them. After your brief closes, you’ll only need to evaluate in detail the number of sellers you said you would consider (for example, just the top 3 responses).

Publish brief to selected sellers

If you want to limit the number of sellers who can respond, at the end of building your brief you can choose to invite one or more sellers to respond. Your brief will still be published in the Marketplace but only the sellers you nominate are able to apply.

Documents and templates

Draft requirements template
Use this template if you want to develop and socialise your requirements before creating a brief in the Digital Marketplace.

Evaluation template
Use this template when evaluating which seller best meets your needs after you’ve shortlisted the responses to your requirements brief.

Deed of Standing Offer
This document details the terms under which sellers are contracted through the Digital Marketplace.

Commonwealth Procurement Rules

All sellers listed in the Digital Marketplace are on the Digital Service Professionals Panel (SON3364729). This panel was established through an open approach to market by the Digital Transformation Office and has complied with the requirements of conducting an open tender as set out in the Commonwealth Procurement Rules. All panel members have executed a Deed of Standing Offer which can be accessed here.

Although the Digital Marketplace makes it easy to comply with the Commonwealth Procurement Rules as a government buyer, you are still responsible for complying with these rules and any Accountable Authority Instructions related to procurement in your agency or issued by the Department of Finance. If you’re not sure of these requirements, please check with the procurement section in your own agency.

Local, state and territory government buyers are welcome to use the Digital Marketplace but should make sure that they comply with any additional procurement rules that apply in their state or territory.

Where can I get help?

You can get help from your agency’s procurement team. You can also contact the Digital Marketplace team through marketplace@digital.gov.au, especially if you have any questions or feedback about the Digital Marketplace itself.

Contact us

The Digital Marketplace is an agile development in its beta stage. This means the site will evolve over time in response to user needs. If you have any questions, feedback or would like to be involved in user research, email us at marketplace@digital.gov.au.
