Buyer User Registration

  1. Users acting on behalf of Buyers must register on the Digital Marketplace at https://marketplace.service.gov.au/ for their Agency to become a registered Buyer and to access the full functionality of the Digital Marketplace. Note, that Buyers and Sellers will have different levels of access to information on the Digital Marketplace. For example, Buyers and their authorised Personnel will be able to see all Seller’s pricing information and the Sellers’ response to the requirements brief. Selllers will not be able to see each other prices or responses to a requirements brief.

Seller User Registration

  1. When a Seller is appointed to the Digital Services Professionals panel they must nominate an Authorised Representative, that is, a contact person authorised by the Seller organisation to act on its behalf. The contact person will be sent an email to activate their Digital Marketplace account. The Seller agrees that the details of the Authorised Representative (the Seller contact person) will be posted on the Digital Marketplace and can be viewed by all registered Buyers. The Seller has obtained the consent of the Seller’s Authorised Representative to have their details being viewed in this way.
  2. The DTO may terminate Your access to the Digital Marketplace at any time for any reason. If the DTO does this, the DTO will not be liable for any Losses of any kind as a consequence of terminating Your access.
  3. Changes in the Seller’s Authorised Representative (contact person) details should be notified to the DTO in the way agreed in the Digital Services Professional panel Deed of Standing Offer.

User responsibilities

  1. You must:
    1. Keep Your login details and password secure
    2. Not share Your login details
    3. Log out of the Digital Marketplace when You are not actively using it.
  2. Any login to the Digital Marketplace that has not been use for 3 months may be suspended.

Sellers responding to a requirements brief

  1. As the Authorised Person (contact person) for a Seller submitting an electronic response to a requirements brief, You agree that You and the Seller are responsible for:
    1. Ensuring that Your technology platform meets the minimum requirements identified, if any, on the Digital Marketplace;
    2. Ensuing Your internet connection is working correctly and in the case of wireless internet connection that the signal is strong and stable;
    3. Making sure you refer to the Digital Marketplace guidance on how to respond to a requirements brief;
    4. Taking all steps to ensure that the response to the requirements brief is free from any Harmful Code or anything else that might affect the operation of the Digital Marketplace or the Buyer’s ICT environment;
    5. Allowing sufficient time for lodging Your response to a requirements brief, including time that may be required to resolve any problems before the closing time of a requirements brief;
    6. Ensuring that the response to the requirements brief does not contain macros, script or executable code of any kind unless that specific material has previously been approved or requested in writing by the Buyer;
    7. Ensuring that responses to the requirements brief comply with all file type, format, naming conventions, size limitations or other requirements specified within the requirements brief or otherwise advised by the Buyer or required by the Digital Marketplace; and
    8. Lodging the Seller requirements brief on time and comply with these Terms of Use.
  2. You and the Seller acknowledge that neither the Buyer nor the DTO will be liable for any Loss, damage, costs or expenses incurred by the User, the Seller or any other person if, for any reason, a response to a requirements brief or any other material or communication relevant to the requirements brief, is not received on time, is corrupted or altered or otherwise is not received as sent, cannot be read or decrypted, or has its security or integrity compromised.
  3. A Seller responding to any requirement brief agrees:
    1. To comply with any file size requirements (if any) detailed in the relevant requirements brief;
    2. To upload the response to a requirements brief from a high level directory on the Seller’s User’s desktop, so as not to impede the upload process;
    3. To ensure that a response to a requirements brief is completely self- contained and that there are no references to hyperlinks. All supporting material that is not directly related to the relevant requirements brief should be provided in accordance with the Buyer’s requirements as detailed in the relevant requirements brief documentation; and
    4. That the response to the requirements brief is authorised by the Seller.
