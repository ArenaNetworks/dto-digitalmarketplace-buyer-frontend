Case study evaluation criteria

Criteria for all services:

  • Work in a multidisciplinary team in an agile delivery environment.
  • Communicate with a wide range of disciplines both internally and externally.
  • Present findings, insights and recommendations to justify decisions.
  • Establish relationships to support delivery of services.

Additional criteria for each service:

  • User research and design
  • Content and publishing
  • Marketing, communications and community engagement
  • Engineering and Development
  • Emerging technology
  • Data science, visualisation and analytics
  • Recruitment
  • Cyber security
  • Strategy, delivery and governance
  • Operations
  • Change management, training and transformation
  • Policy and law
  • Products

Content & publishing

Content and publishing covers any business that provides or manages content.

Case studies must demonstrate one or more of the following:

  • Providing content that meets user need, in a format that maximises comprehension - such as plain language copy, photography, illustration, interactive media and video.
  • Creating content for different platforms and devices.
  • Creating and/or following style guides.
  • Experience in content publishing systems, plus auditing and governance of content.
  • Utilising metrics and user feedback to define and improve content.

Emerging technology

Emerging technologists develop or adapt ideas and technology for commercial application.

Emerging technology case studies must demonstrate:

Solving a business problem through the research, prototyping or commercial application of an emerging technology. Example technologies include, but are not limited to:

  • Artificial intelligence
  • Robotics
  • Wearables
  • Beacons, BLE, accessory port, ADK
  • VR, AR, Kinect, NUI, or machine vision technologies.

Data science, visualisation & analytics

Data science brings analytical rigour and statistical methods to the challenges of measuring quality, improving service and modelling behaviour.

Data science case studies must demonstrate:

  • Solving difficult, non-routine analysis problems, working with large, complex data sets
  • Experience with statistical methods such as parametric and nonparametric significance testing
  • Experience with statistical software (e.g., R, Julia, MATLAB, pandas) and database languages (e.g. SQL).
  • Providing insight or recommendations (e.g. cost-benefit, forecasting, experiment analysis) through visual displays of quantitative information.
