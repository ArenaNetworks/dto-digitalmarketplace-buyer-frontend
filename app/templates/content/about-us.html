About the Digital Marketplace

We're transforming how government buys digital and technology services.

Our purpose

The Digital Marketplace is an initiative under the National Innovation and Science Agenda. Our purpose is to make it easier for smaller businesses to compete for the government’s $5 billion a year spend on information and communications technology (ICT) products and services.

To do this we’re building an ecosystem where government buyers and sellers can connect and have the procurement interactions they need — at the level of simplicity or complexity appropriate to their circumstances — and to make the outcomes of those interactions more transparent.

Our progress

The Digital Marketplace is an agile development in its beta stage. This means the site will evolve over time in response to user needs. If you have any questions, feedback or would like to be involved in user research, contact us at marketplace@digital.gov.au.

How we comply with the procurement rules

All sellers listed in the Digital Marketplace are on the Digital Service Professionals Panel (SON3364729). This panel was established through an open approach to market by the Digital Transformation Agency and has complied with the requirements of conducting an open tender as set out in the Commonwealth Procurement Rules. All panel members have executed a Deed of Standing Offer which can be accessed here.

Although the Digital Marketplace makes it easy to comply with the Commonwealth Procurement Rules as a government buyer, you are still responsible for complying with these rules and any Accountable Authority Instructions related to procurement in your agency or issued by the Department of Finance. If you’re not sure of these requirements, please check with the procurement section in your own agency.

Local, state and territory government buyers are welcome to use the Digital Marketplace but should make sure that they comply with any additional procurement rules that apply in their state or territory.
